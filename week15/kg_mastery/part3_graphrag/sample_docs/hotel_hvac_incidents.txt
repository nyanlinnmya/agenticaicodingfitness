Hotel HVAC Incident Log — Energy & Facilities

Room R305 has repeatedly run hot over the past week. The HVAC compressor in
R305 is overheating during afternoon peak hours, pushing room temperature above
28C and driving energy consumption above 5 kWh. Maintenance suspects a failing
condenser fan and a clogged air filter.

Room R101 and Room R203 also run hot but to a lesser degree. R101's thermostat
appears miscalibrated, reporting 2C below the actual room temperature, so the
HVAC under-cools the space. R203 shares a riser with R305 and inherits some of
its heat load.

Two HIGH-severity alerts remain unresolved. Alert AL1 was triggered by the
R305 HVAC unit for sustained over-temperature. Alert AL2 was triggered by the
R203 sensor for abnormal energy draw. Both alerts are flagged for the energy
optimisation team.

Recommended actions: replace the R305 condenser fan, recalibrate the R101
thermostat, and schedule HVAC pre-cooling before the afternoon occupancy peak
to flatten the energy curve. Estimated combined saving is roughly 3 to 4 kWh
per day across the three rooms.
